Conversion Insight

In the canonical qubit parameterization, θ/2 compresses the state vector so that |0⟩ and |1⟩ sit at antipodal poles even as θ remains within [0, π]. Toggling conversion remaps every reference point to interpret θ directly, stretching the sphere's texture and animating the grid into its new configuration.

  • Base lattice points refresh with hues derived from their azimuthal angle.
  • The active state marker pulses with the same palette as the grid point it co-locates with.
  • Both modes preserve normalization: |cos(·)|² + |sin(·)|² = 1.

How to explore

  1. Drag around the sphere to understand how θ (latitude) and φ (longitude) sculpt the state.
  2. Use conversion to observe how halving versus preserving θ affects pole placement and the |0⟩/|1⟩ basis.
  3. Track the amplitude panel to see the complex coefficients update in real time.
